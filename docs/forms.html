Forms

Inputs, selects, checkboxes, radios, and toggles sized for accessibility and built around a shared .wire-field shell that pairs label, control, hint, and error.

Anatomy

A form field stacks a label, the control, and optional helper or error text. Group related fields in a <fieldset class="wire-fieldset">; lay out the whole form in .wire-form with optional .wire-form__row for side-by-side fields.

Default field
As it appears on your insurance card.

When to use

  • Use .wire-input for short single-line text; .wire-textarea for multi-line free text.
  • Use .wire-select for a small, fixed list of mutually exclusive options.
  • Use .wire-radio when one option must be chosen and all options should be visible.
  • Use .wire-toggle for an immediate on/off setting that takes effect without a save step.

When not to use

  • Do not use a .wire-select for more than ~10 options — use a search or autocomplete instead.
  • Do not use a .wire-toggle when the change requires confirmation — use a checkbox paired with a submit button.
  • Do not place required asterisks without the .wire-field__label-required modifier — keep the marker consistent across the system.

Variants

Every primitive ships a default, hover, focus-visible, disabled, and .is-invalid state. Combine with .wire-field to attach label, hint, and error.

Accessibility

  • Every control must have a programmatically associated <label>; do not rely on placeholders for labelling.
  • Mark required fields with .wire-field__label-required and the required attribute on the control.
  • Link error text via aria-describedby on the input; the .wire-field__error announces the constraint.
  • All controls meet a 44px touch target. Checkbox/radio labels have padding to expand the hit area.
  • Focus is visible on every interactive control via a 2px focus ring with offset.

Show snippet 
<form class="wire-form">
  <div class="wire-field">
    <label class="wire-field__label wire-field__label-required" for="email">Email</label>
    <input id="email" class="wire-input" type="email" required>
    <span class="wire-field__hint">We will send a confirmation here.</span>
  </div>
  <button class="wire-button" type="submit">Submit</button>
</form>
