Content blocks

The middle of a page is built from a small set of reliable blocks: feature grids, two-column splits, accordions, tabs, callouts, quotes, and timelines. Compose them; do not invent new ones.

Feature grid

A 2-, 3-, or 4-up grid of features, services, or value propositions. Optional --bordered turns each feature into a card-like tile.

Accordion

Built on native <details>/<summary>. Add data-wire-accordion="single" to enforce one-open behavior.

FAQ — single-open
What should I bring to my first appointment?
A photo ID, your insurance card, a list of current medications, recent imaging or test results if you have them, and any referral notes from your physician.
Do I need a referral?
For most outpatient services, yes. Inpatient admissions are coordinated directly with your treating hospital.
Is parking available?
Valet and self-parking are available at all locations. Patients receive a daily discount validated at check-in.

Accessibility

  • Accordion uses native <details>, which screen readers announce as expand/collapse without extra ARIA.
  • Tabs require role="tablist", role="tab", aria-selected, aria-controls, and arrow-key navigation (wired by data-wire-tabs).
  • Quote uses <figure> with <blockquote> and <figcaption> so attribution stays semantically attached.
  • Timeline marks the visual bullets aria-hidden via CSS; the ordered list itself carries semantics.
  • Callouts are not landmarks — do not wrap them in <aside> unless the content is genuinely tangential.
