The system at a glance

Six things that hold every page together.

01

Grayscale only

Hierarchy from weight, scale, and space — never hue. Wireframes stay about structure and content.

02

Tokens first

Every value lives in tokens.css. Components reference; nothing hardcodes.

03

Plain HTML & CSS

No build step. Open a file in a browser, it works — portable to Figma Make, Claude artifacts, client handoff.

04

WCAG 2.2 AA

Contrast tested. Focus rings on everything. Targets ≥44px. Reduced-motion respected.

05

BEM-ish & prefixed

Every class starts with p- for components, u- for utilities. No collisions when embedded.

06

Content variations, not style overrides

Variants are constrained. Designers swap copy and structure — the system stays consistent.

Display + lead + body

Eyebrow label

A confident headline. With an editorial moment.

A lead paragraph carries the next layer of information — short, important, slightly muted so it doesn't compete with the headline.

Body text sits in the workhorse role. It's set in a generous size for screen reading, with relaxed line height. Sentences and paragraphs should stay readable up to about 70 characters wide — the system enforces this with a default max-inline-size on p.

Accordion
What should I bring to my first appointment?
A photo ID, your insurance card, a list of current medications, recent imaging or test results if you have them, and any referral notes from your physician.
Do I need a referral?
For most outpatient services, yes. Inpatient admissions are coordinated directly with your treating hospital.
Is parking available?
Valet and self-parking are available at all locations. Patients receive a daily discount validated at check-in.
