Purpose

We're testing whether a wearable closed-loop neural stimulator, paired with 12 weeks of intensive physical therapy, can restore voluntary movement and improve standing balance in people with chronic complete spinal cord injury — the population most often told there's nothing more to try.

The device is non-implantable: electrodes sit on the skin over the lumbar spine. A closed-loop algorithm decodes intended movement from electromyography signals and delivers a tailored stimulation pulse. Earlier Phase 1 results in nine participants were published in Nature Medicine in 2024.

What's involved

  1. Screening 2 visits, 2 weeks
  2. Baseline 1 week of measurement
  3. Intervention 12 weeks, 5 days/week
  4. Follow-up 6 visits over 24 weeks

Risks & benefits

Possible risks

  • Skin irritation under the electrodes — usually mild, resolves between sessions.
  • Muscle soreness — some participants report soreness for the first 1–2 weeks as muscles re-engage.
  • Autonomic dysreflexia — uncommon but possible; the team monitors blood pressure during every session.
  • Time commitment can be physically and emotionally taxing.
  • The intervention may not work for you. Most published research on closed-loop SCI stimulation shows variable response.

Possible benefits

  • Some volitional movement in muscles below the level of injury (the primary outcome of Phase 1).
  • Improved standing balance and trunk control.
  • Reduced spasticity.
  • You contribute to research that may help others with SCI — Phase 3 trials and FDA submission depend on Phase 2 data like this.

Honest note. This is a Phase 2 trial — we're studying whether it works, not delivering a proven treatment. The device is not FDA-approved for this use.

FAQ

Do I need to switch my care team to enroll?+

No. Your current providers stay your providers. We coordinate with them — we don't replace them.

Will my insurance be billed?+

Research-related costs are covered by the trial budget. Standard care visits that would have happened anyway may be billed to your insurance. The coordinator goes through this with you at screening.

Can I leave the study?+

Always, at any time, for any reason or no reason. Your care at Riverside is not affected.

Will I get the results of my own testing?+

Yes. You'll receive a summary of your individual outcomes at the end of the study, and a copy of the aggregated study results when they're published.
