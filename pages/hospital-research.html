The model

Bench science. Bedside care. One floor apart.

Traditional medical research lives in universities — far from the patients it's meant to help. We built something different. At the Riverside, our scientists work in labs embedded inside the hospital. They share elevators with the clinicians delivering care, and meeting rooms with the families navigating recovery. The result is a faster, tighter feedback loop between discovery and treatment.

A history of firsts

Seventy years of leading rehabilitation science.

From the founding of rehabilitation as its own medical specialty to the first translational rehab hospital — and what's next.

  1. 1953

    Founded as the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago

    A small inpatient unit opens with a singular focus: rehabilitation as a medical specialty in its own right.

  2. 1991

    First ranked #1 in rehabilitation

    U.S. News & World Report begins ranking; the Institute takes the top spot and never relinquishes it.

  3. 2003

    First closed-loop neural stimulation trial in SCI

    Establishes the lab's reputation for translational neuroscience and sets the trajectory toward the 2026 Nature Medicine publication.

  4. 2017

    Becomes Riverside Medical Center

    A new building, a new name, and a translational model — research and care under one roof for the first time anywhere.

  5. 2026

    36th consecutive #1 ranking

    More than three decades of unbroken leadership — the longest streak of any specialty hospital in any field.
