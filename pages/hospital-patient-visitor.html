Planning your visit.

Practical answers for first-time patients and the people supporting them — hours, parking, what to bring, and who to call when you're not sure.

If this is a medical emergency, call 911. Riverside Medical Center does not have an emergency department. For urgent questions about an existing patient, page the on-call physician at 312.238.1000.

Before your visit

Most appointments start with a call from our intake team a few days ahead. Here's what to expect.

  1. Intake call (3–5 days before). A coordinator confirms the appointment, reviews insurance, and gathers your medical history.
  2. Insurance verification. We submit pre-authorization on your behalf. You'll get a written estimate before the visit.
  3. Pre-visit forms. We email a secure link to complete forms online. If you'd rather use paper, arrive 30 minutes early.
  4. Day-before reminder. Text or call — your choice.

Day of your visit

Arrival

Plan to arrive 15 minutes before your appointment. Check in at the main lobby on the first floor.

Wayfinding

Concierge desks are staffed 7 AM – 7 PM. Color-coded elevators correspond to floors. Ask for an escort if you'd like one.

Parking & transit

By car

Valet at 355 East Erie (main entrance). $18/day with appointment validation.

By transit

CTA Red Line, Chicago station. Two blocks east. Step-free entrance at the main lobby.

Accessible parking

32 ADA spaces on level 1 of the garage. Direct elevator access to the lobby.

What to bring

Required

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • List of current medications and dosages
  • Referral or order, if your provider sent one
  • Names and contact info for your other doctors

Helpful

  • Loose, comfortable clothing for movement assessments
  • Closed-toe shoes you walk in regularly
  • Glasses, hearing aids, mobility devices you use
  • A family member or friend, if you'd like one with you
  • Notebook or phone for taking notes

Frequently asked

How do I cancel or reschedule? +

Call 312.238.8000 at least 24 hours ahead, or message your care team in MyChart. Late cancellations may carry a fee depending on your insurance.

What insurance do you accept? +

Most major commercial plans, Medicare, and Illinois Medicaid. We verify coverage before every visit and call you with an estimate if there's anything you'll owe out of pocket.

Will I need an interpreter? +

Free interpretation in 100+ spoken languages and ASL. Tell intake when you book — or any time during your stay. No documentation required.

Can my family attend appointments? +

Yes. Bring whoever helps you make decisions about your care — partner, parent, adult child, friend. Family meetings can be scheduled separately at no additional cost.

How do I get my medical records? +

Through MyChart immediately. For copies sent to another provider, call Health Information Management at 312.238.1080 — typical turnaround is two business days.
