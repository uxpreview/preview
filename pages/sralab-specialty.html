Overview

For more than 70 years, our team has specialized in spinal cord injury rehabilitation. We treat patients at every stage — from the days after injury to decades into recovery — across inpatient, day rehab, and outpatient settings.

Our approach combines intensive physical and occupational therapy with applied research. Patients in our program often participate in studies testing the next generation of neural stimulation, robotic-assisted training, and adaptive technology. The result: care plans that get more precise every year.

How care works

Three settings, one care team. We match the program to the patient — not the other way around.

Inpatient rehabilitation

For patients in the early weeks after injury. Round-the-clock care from a multidisciplinary team — physiatrists, nurses, PTs, OTs, speech therapists, psychologists, and recreational therapists — on a unit designed entirely for SCI rehabilitation. Most stays run 14 to 21 days.

Therapy hours
3+ hours of focused therapy daily, 6 days a week.
Family involvement
Caregiver training built into the schedule, not an afterthought.
Equipment
Access to robotic exoskeletons, functional electrical stimulation, and adaptive technology.

Day rehabilitation

A bridge between inpatient and home. Patients spend half-days at the AbilityLab, 3 to 5 days a week, getting the intensity of inpatient therapy while sleeping in their own beds. Typical programs run 4 to 8 weeks.

Outpatient therapy

Targeted physical, occupational, and speech therapy for patients living at home. Visits are scheduled flexibly around work, school, and family.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a referral?
Inpatient admissions are coordinated directly with your treating hospital. For outpatient or day-rehab evaluation, a referral from your physician is typically required by your insurance.
How long is the typical inpatient stay?
For SCI, the average inpatient stay is 21 days, though it varies based on the level of injury and individual goals. Your care team will set a target length of stay during the first 48 hours.
What insurance do you accept?
We accept most major insurance plans, Medicare, and Medicaid. Our financial counselors will verify coverage before your visit and help you understand any out-of-pocket costs.
Will I be in a research study?
Participation is always optional. Patients who are eligible may be invited to enroll in active studies, but care is never contingent on participation.
