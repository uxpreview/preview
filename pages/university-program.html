Overview

Cognitive science asks what is arguably the oldest interesting question — what is a mind? — and answers it with whatever tool fits the question: a brain scan, a Bayesian model, a corpus of recorded speech, a philosophical thought experiment, a chimpanzee study.

The Northgate program is anchored in computational and experimental methods. Every major takes a year of statistics and a year of programming, and runs a senior capstone with one of our 22 affiliated faculty. Graduates go to medical school, PhD programs, machine-learning labs, and policy think tanks at roughly equal rates — the major prepares you for the question you haven't picked yet.

New for fall 2026. A four-course AI & Mind concentration sits inside the major, drawing on faculty across CS, Linguistics, and Philosophy. No extra credits required.

What you'll study

Twelve courses in the major — four foundations, four track electives, four capstone-track. Every student also takes a yearlong methods sequence in their second year.

The four foundations

  • COGS 110. Introduction to Cognitive Science — a tour of the six disciplines through one running question (how do we understand language?).
  • COGS 210. Computational Models of Mind — programming required; you'll build small models in Python and reason about their behavior.
  • COGS 220. The Brain and Cognition — neuroanatomy with a focus on the systems underlying perception, attention, memory, and language.
  • COGS 250. Philosophy of Mind — consciousness, intentionality, the hard problem, and the recent computational rebuttals.

Four tracks

  • Computational — modeling, machine learning, computational linguistics.
  • Linguistic — syntax, semantics, phonology, language acquisition, sociolinguistics.
  • Neuroscientific — cognitive neuroscience, neuroimaging methods, computational neuroscience.
  • Philosophical — philosophy of mind, language, action, with a foothold in formal logic.

Senior capstone

Two semesters, one faculty mentor, one substantial piece of work. Roughly a third of capstones go to publication or conference presentation; the rest become the writing sample for graduate-school applications.

FAQ

Do I need a math background to declare?+

You need to be willing to take a year of statistics and a year of programming, both of which assume only high-school algebra. Students who place out of these are welcome to but it's not required.

Can I double major?+

Roughly 40% of majors do. The most common combinations are CS (joint major), Philosophy, Psychology, and Linguistics. Joint majors share courses, reducing the credit overhead.

Is the senior capstone required?+

Yes. You pick a faculty mentor in the spring of your third year and propose a project. Two-thirds of capstones become writing samples for grad school; about a third reach publication or conference presentation.

What if I'm coming from a community college?+

Northgate accepts transfers each fall and spring. The Office of Undergraduate Admissions and the program director will work with you on credit articulation; up to 64 credits may transfer.
