Density

Spacing comes from one scale. The system leans comfortable by default — generous spacing and large targets suit healthcare and older audiences — and tightens only where dense data genuinely earns it.

One spacing scale

Every gap, pad, and margin references a --space-* token; nothing is hand-typed. The scale is roughly geometric, so steps stay visually distinct, and it is shared across every component — the same rhythm runs through a form, a card, and a page header.

Tokenrempx
--space-3xs0.1252
--space-2xs0.254
--space-xs0.58
--space-sm0.7512
--space-md116
--space-lg1.524
--space-xl232
--space-2xl348
--space-3xl464
--space-4xl696
--space-5xl8128

Comfortable by default

The resting density is roomy: larger body text, open line-height, and generous padding around controls and content. In healthcare and higher-ed the reader often skews older, is anxious, or is on a phone in a waiting room — comfortable density lowers the effort of every interaction.

Resist the urge to compress in order to fit more above the fold. Whitespace is doing work: it groups related things, separates unrelated ones, and gives the eye somewhere to rest. (Convention — grouping by proximity is a Gestalt principle, not a measured number.)

When to increase density

Some views earn a tighter setting: data tables, comparison grids, dashboards, and internal admin tools, where seeing more rows at once is the task. There, step one level down the scale — say row padding from --space-lg to --space-md — and hold it consistently across the view.

Density is local

It is a per-region decision, not a global theme. A dense comparison table can live happily on an otherwise comfortable page; the page does not have to commit to one density everywhere.

Targets hold their size

Tightening density compresses spacing, never the hit area. WCAG 2.2 sets a 24 px minimum target (2.5.8 Target Size (Minimum), AA — a requirement); this system holds the stronger 44 px target (2.5.5 Enhanced / platform convention) because of who uses these pages.

So when a row gets denser, the visible padding can shrink but the clickable region of a control stays at least 44 px. Visual density and target size are two separate dials.

Do and don't

Do
Compose spacing from the token scale so the same rhythm repeats everywhere.
Do
Step density down by one level for genuine data-dense views, consistently.
Don't
Compress a comfortable reading or form page just to raise the fold.
Don't
Shrink a tap target to fit a tighter row — compress the padding instead.
