Healthcare overview

How the system applies to hospital and clinic work — who you are designing for, what they came to do, and the patterns that carry the most weight. A starting playbook for healthcare engagements.

Who you're designing for

Healthcare audiences arrive with real stakes and often under stress. They skew older, frequently navigate on a phone, and include people with low vision, motor, or literacy needs. Design for the hardest case and everyone benefits.

  • Patients — finding care, booking, preparing for a visit, managing follow-up.
  • Family and caregivers — acting on someone else's behalf, often remotely.
  • Referring providers — a smaller, task-focused audience with different language.

What matters most

A handful of tasks account for most healthcare visits. Make them the spine of the IA.

Top taskWhere it lives
Find a doctor or serviceDirectory + specialty landings.
Plan a visitLocation, hours, parking, what to bring.
Book or contactScheduling, phone, portal sign-in.
Get help nowAlways-present emergency path.

Patterns to reach for

The system ships healthcare-shaped parts; start from these rather than building anew.

  • Directory flow — the Find a doctor template and demo.
  • Visit planning — the Plan your visit template.
  • Trust and logistics cues — wait time, insurance indicator.
  • Safety — the always-reachable emergency line (see Alerts & emergencies).

Tone and accessibility

Healthcare raises the floor on clarity and accessibility. Lean on the dedicated playbooks: Senior-friendly for older audiences, Health literacy for comprehension, and Care-journey wayfinding for the multi-step path. Keep language plain (Plain language) and density comfortable (Density).

Do
Build the IA around the few tasks that bring people here.
Don't
Bury "find care" or the emergency path beneath institutional content.
