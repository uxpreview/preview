Shape & radius

Corner radii and border weights — the shape language. The radius scale is generous on purpose: soft corners read as approachable and modern at wireframe fidelity.

The radius scale

Seven steps plus a pill. Pick the smallest radius that reads as intentional at the element's size — large radii on small elements look like mistakes, and vice versa.

--radius-2xl2rem (32px)Hero / feature surfaces
--radius-pill999pxPills, badges, avatars, toggles

Border weights

Three weights. Thin is the default hairline; heavier weights carry emphasis and selected states. Strong borders must clear 3:1 non-text contrast (WCAG 1.4.11), which is why bordered surfaces use --color-border-strong.

--border-thin1pxHairline borders, dividers (default)

--border-thick3pxHeavy emphasis, active indicators

Applying shape

Match radius to element scale and role, and keep it consistent within a family (all inputs share one radius, all cards another).

ElementRadius
Inputs, buttons--radius-sm
Menus, popovers, tooltips--radius-md
Cards, panels--radius-lg
Modals, drawers, sheets--radius-xl
Badges, pills, avatars, toggles--radius-pill
