Insurance indicator

Beta

Whether a provider or location accepts a visitor's plan — one of the first things people check before booking care. A marker icon, a plain status label, and the plan detail. The status is always stated in words; the icon and emphasis only reinforce it.

  • css/components/insurance-indicator.css
  • Molecule · no JavaScript

Live demo

Three states — accepted, not accepted, and unverified — each told in words, with a distinct marker shape and a step of emphasis.

Overview

"Do they take my insurance?" is one of the first questions a patient asks, and a wrong answer is expensive. This indicator states the answer plainly and never hides it behind a colour: the label says the status outright, the marker shape reinforces it, and the detail names the plans or the caveat.

When to use

  • On a provider or location page, near the booking action.
  • In a find-a-doctor result row, so coverage is visible while comparing.
  • Anywhere a plan-acceptance answer affects whether someone proceeds.

When not to use

  • Don't assert "accepts your plan" unless the plan is actually known; default to "Verify your coverage".
  • For a generic one-word tag of status, use a Badge; this is a fuller statement with detail.
  • Don't use it to list every accepted plan exhaustively — link to a full coverage page for that.

Anatomy

A marker icon beside a body that stacks the status label over its supporting detail.

icon · label · detail In-network — accepts your plan Aetna · Blue Cross Blue Shield · Cigna
PartClassRole
Block.wire-insurance-indicatorThe row. Carries the emphasis variant.
Icon.wire-insurance-indicator__iconA marker whose shape (check / slash / query) reinforces the state. Decorative.
Body.wire-insurance-indicator__bodyStacks the label over the detail.
Label.wire-insurance-indicator__labelThe status, stated in words. The load-bearing part.
Detail.wire-insurance-indicator__detailPlan names, a caveat, or a verify prompt.

States & emphasis

There is no green/red here. The state lives in the label wording and the marker shape; --muted lowers emphasis for the non-affirmative states so the affirmative one reads first.

StateMarker & classWording
AcceptedCheck, full emphasis (.wire-insurance-indicator)"In-network — accepts your plan".
Not acceptedSlash, .wire-insurance-indicator--muted"Out-of-network for your plan".
UnverifiedQuery, .wire-insurance-indicator--muted"Verify your coverage".

Composability

A self-contained statement that drops into the places a coverage answer is needed.

  • On a provider card — beneath the name and specialty, above the booking button.
  • In a result row — the accepted state visible while a user scans options.
  • With a callout — pair the "verify" state with a link to a coverage-check tool.
  • Not a filter — to let users filter by plan, that is a Tag in a facet panel, not this.

In context

On a provider summary, beside the booking action.

Provider summary
Dr. Elena Ruiz, MD Interventional Cardiology · Riverside Heart Institute In-network — accepts your plan Based on Aetna PPO · Check your plan

Do / Don't

Do

Verify your coverage Plan acceptance can change — confirm before your visit.

When the plan isn't known, say so and prompt a check. Honest uncertainty beats a confident wrong answer.

Don't

Covered

A bare "Covered" with no plan and no caveat over-promises. Name the basis or prompt a check.

Content guidelines

  • State the status in the label; never let the icon or emphasis be the only signal.
  • Say what the answer is based on ("Based on Aetna PPO") so it's verifiable.
  • Default to "Verify your coverage" whenever the plan is unknown.
  • Keep the detail to the relevant plans or one caveat — link out for the full list.

Measurements & structure

PropertyValue
Layoutinline-flex, top-aligned; icon fixed, body a stacked column.
Icon1.25rem square (fixed component geometry in rem), currentColor.
Marker shapesCheck (accepted), slash (not accepted), query (unverified) — the shape, not colour, distinguishes them.
Emphasis--muted drops the icon and label to --color-text-muted; the detail is already muted.

States

No interactive states. The three coverage states are content (marker shape + wording) plus the --muted emphasis step, documented in Usage › States & emphasis — not hover/focus/active states.

JavaScript

None in the component. Resolving which plan the visitor has, and which state to render, is the application's job. No data-wire-* hooks. If coverage is determined after load, swap the rendered state and announce it in an aria-live region.

</span>

ARIA & semantics

  • The marker icon is decorative: aria-hidden="true", focusable="false". The label text is the status.
  • Never encode acceptance with colour or icon shape alone — the wording must state it, which is what makes it safe in grayscale.
  • Keep the label and detail as adjacent text so the statement reads as a whole.
  • If coverage resolves after load, render the new state inside an aria-live="polite" region so the change is announced.

Keyboard interactions

None for the indicator itself — it is read, not operated. Any "check your plan" link inside the detail is a normal link with its own focus ring and tab stop.

WCAG 2.2 criteria mapped

SCHow Insurance indicator satisfies it
1.1.1 Non-text ContentThe marker icon is decorative and hidden; the meaning is in the label and detail text.
1.4.1 Use of ColorAcceptance is in the wording and the marker shape, never colour. Grayscale guarantees no colour-only meaning.
1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum)Label is --color-text and detail --color-text-muted on the surface; both clear 4.5:1.
4.1.3 Status MessagesA state resolved after load is announced via an aria-live region.

Screen reader notes

  • The statement reads as its text, e.g. "In-network — accepts your plan. Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna"; the marker is skipped.
  • Because the status is in words, the accepted and not-accepted states are unambiguous without seeing the icon.
  • The "verify" state reads its prompt, guiding the user to confirm rather than implying a definite answer.
  • A post-load change inside aria-live="polite" is announced once, without moving focus.
