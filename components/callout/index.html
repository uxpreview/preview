Callout

Stable

A boxed editorial aside that lifts a single message out of the page — an enrolling study, a closure notice, a referral pathway. Use it sparingly: at most one per long page.

  • css/components/callout.css
  • Since v1.0

Live demo

An eyebrow frames the message, a title carries it, body copy supports it, and up to two actions follow.

Default
Now enrolling

Study on robotic-assisted gait training

We are recruiting adults 18–65 who have experienced a stroke in the last 12 months.

See eligibility Contact the team

Anatomy

PartClassRole
Container.wire-calloutThe boxed region (plus a variant modifier).
Eyebrow.wire-callout__eyebrowAn uppercase kicker that frames the message.
Title.wire-callout__titleThe headline — set on a real heading at the level that fits the page.
Body.wire-callout__bodySupporting copy.
Actions.wire-callout__actionsUp to two buttons.

Variants

Four surfaces plus a denser inline form. The surface changes; the structure doesn't.

Subtle
Note

Memorial Day hours

Our Streeterville location will be closed Monday, May 25. Outpatient visits resume Tuesday.

Bordered
For clinicians

Referral pathways

Step-by-step referral templates for inpatient, day rehab, and outpatient services.

View pathways
Inverse
Speak with a navigator

Not sure where to start?

A care navigator can help you understand your options and the next step.

Get in touch
Inline (in-content)

Heads up: This program is currently accepting new patients on a four-week wait.

When to use

  • Use a callout to lift one editorial message out of the flow — an enrolling study, a closure, a navigator prompt.
  • At most one boxed callout per long page; more than one and none of them stands out.
  • It isn't a system message. For validation, status, or an urgent alert use a Banner; for a person's words use Quote.
  • The inline variant is a denser, left-bar aside for a short note inside running content.

Do / Don't

Do

  • Keep it to one message and at most two actions.
  • Lead with an eyebrow that frames what follows.
  • Reserve the inverse surface for a single high-emphasis prompt.

Don't

  • Stack several boxed callouts in a row.
  • Use a callout for errors or urgent status — that's a Banner.
  • Hide body copy here that belongs in the page itself.

Surfaces

Each variant re-maps the callout's three custom properties; nothing else changes.

VariantSurface
Default--color-bg-muted, no border.
Subtle--color-bg-subtle, muted body.
BorderedTransparent fill, --color-border-strong outline.
Inverse--color-bg-inverse on --color-text-inverse; focus + secondary button re-mapped for the dark surface.
Inline--color-bg-subtle with a --color-accent leading bar; denser, row layout.

PropertyToken
Padding--space-xl (boxed) · --space-md --space-lg (inline)

Snippet

<div class="wire-callout">
  <span class="wire-callout__eyebrow">Now enrolling</span>
  <h3 class="wire-callout__title">Study on robotic-assisted gait training</h3>
  <p class="wire-callout__body">Recruiting adults 18–65 who had a stroke in the last year.</p>
  <div class="wire-callout__actions">
    <a class="wire-button wire-button--sm" href="/eligibility">See eligibility</a>
  </div>
</div>

Semantics

  • A callout is a styled region, not a landmark or an alert. Don't add role="alert" — it's editorial, not a live status message (that's a Banner).
  • The title is a real heading: choose the level that continues the page outline rather than skipping one.
  • The eyebrow is decorative emphasis; keep the meaning in the title and body, not in the kicker alone.

Contrast on the inverse surface

  • The inverse variant flips text to --color-text-inverse and re-maps --color-focus so the focus ring stays visible on the dark fill (WCAG 2.4.7).
  • It also re-maps the secondary button's border and text so the button reads on dark instead of vanishing.
  • All five surfaces hold AA text contrast in both light and dark themes.

Actions & targets

  • Action buttons are real <a>/<button> elements from the button component, so they carry a 44px target and a visible focus ring.
  • Keep to two actions; a callout is a prompt, not a toolbar.
