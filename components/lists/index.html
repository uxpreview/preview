Lists

Stable

Three text-first list patterns: a linked list of whole-row destinations, a definition list of labelled facts, and an article list for editorial indexes. Each is plain semantic markup with a typographic treatment.

  • css/components/list.css
  • Since v1.0

Linked list

Large clickable rows for a category index — the whole row is the link, with an optional meta count on the right and an arrow affordance.

With meta
  • Spinal cord injury14 conditions
  • Stroke22 conditions
  • Pediatric rehabilitation27 conditions

Definition list

Term and description pairs for labelled facts. Single column by default; a two-column modifier sets the term beside its description at wider widths.

Single column
Inpatient
Round-the-clock care delivered while the patient lives on the unit. Average stay: 14–21 days.
Day rehab
Half-day intensive therapy, 3–5 days a week, for patients who can live at home.
Outpatient
Targeted physical, occupational, or speech therapy scheduled flexibly.
Two column
Patient programs
Inpatient, day rehab, outpatient, and telehealth across four locations.
Research focus
Translational rehabilitation science — bench to bedside in months, not years.
Insurance
Most major plans accepted; financial counselors available pre-visit.

Article list

Editorial entries with a meta column (category + date) and a content column (title + excerpt). Each item is a real article with a linked heading.

News / research index
  • Research Apr 18, 2026

    Robotic exoskeletons improve gait speed in chronic stroke

    A new randomized trial led by Dr. Okafor's team shows sustained gains six months post-intervention.

  • Story Apr 04, 2026

    A father, a son, and the first steps after a spinal cord injury

    Daniel's family rallied behind him for six months of inpatient rehab. Here's what carried them through.

Choosing a list

  • Linked list — a category index where every entry is a destination, optionally with a count.
  • Definition list — labelled facts: visit types, insurance, credentials.
  • Article list — news, research, or story indexes where each item has a category and date.
  • When entries need images or rich previews, reach for a Card grid instead.

Do / Don't

Do

  • Make the whole row the link in a linked list.
  • Use real dl / dt / dd for term–description pairs.
  • Give each article a real heading and a linked title.

Don't

  • Use a definition list as a two-column layout hack — use .u-grid.
  • Use a linked list when items need images — use cards.
  • Nest article lists; keep one flat sequence per section.

Structure

ListMarkupLayout
Linkedul > li > full-row aHairline rows; row is a flex link with a trailing arrow and optional meta.
Definitiondl / dt / ddGrid; one column by default, term-beside-description with --two-col at 48em.
Articleul > li > articleMeta column (12rem) + content column at 48em; stacks below.

Tokens

PropertyToken
Hairlines--border-thin --color-border
Linked row--text-lg, --weight-medium, padding-block --space-lg
Deflist term--text-sm, --tracking-wide, --color-text-muted
Article title--text-xl → --text-2xl at 48em
Excerpt measure70ch, --color-text-muted

Snippet

<ul class="wire-linked-list" role="list">
  <li class="wire-linked-list__item">
    <a class="wire-linked-list__link" href="/sci">
      <span>Spinal cord injury</span><span class="wire-linked-list__meta">14 conditions</span>
    </a>
  </li>
</ul>

<dl class="wire-deflist wire-deflist--two-col">
  <dt class="wire-deflist__term">Inpatient</dt>
  <dd class="wire-deflist__desc">Round-the-clock care on the unit.</dd>
</dl>

Semantics

  • Linked and article lists are real <ul>s with role="list" — the role restores list semantics that list-style: none can strip in some browsers.
  • The definition list uses dl / dt / dd, so each term is programmatically tied to its description.
  • Article titles are real headings with a link; the date is a <time>.

Interaction

  • The linked-list row is one large link — a generous --space-lg block padding gives a tall, easy target.
  • The trailing arrow and the hover shift are affordances drawn in CSS; they aren't separate controls.
  • Focus shows the global ring on the row link and the article title link.

Readability

  • Article excerpts cap at a 70ch measure so the index stays scannable.
  • Code-style terms in a definition list keep their real casing — the uppercase treatment applies only to label terms.
